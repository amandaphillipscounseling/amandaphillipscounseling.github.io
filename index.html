Supporting you to navigate life's challenges with compassion and connection.

You have a dream of your family and your life, and currently it's not where you want it to be. Meltdowns, school avoidance, isolation, and worry characterize the feel of your home when it should be a place of joy and peace. Maybe your child has gone through some really difficult events, or the connection you had together feels like it's in danger of falling apart. You want yourself and your loved ones to get back to a place of thriving and enjoying life. Therapy can be the place to get your child and family the support it needs.

I help you and your child get to the root of the matter, and work through it while learning skills, feeling understood, and processing the issues that keep getting in the way of thriving.

Meet Amanda

Amanda Phillips, Associate Professional Clinical Counselor, APCC #14941

I provide a warm, collaborative, and trauma-informed approach grounded in attachment, humanistic, and family systems principles. I specialize in work with youth and teens struggling with big emotions, anxiety, depression, or behavioral issues, and parents feeling exhausted by recurring patterns at home. Every behavior tells a story. Whether your child is melting down, withdrawing, or struggling to cope, my goal isn't simply to stop the behavior - it's to understand the emotions and unmet needs beneath it so lasting change can occur.

I believe therapy works best when people feel truly seen, accepted, and understood. My approach is warm, collaborative, and grounded in evidence-based practices tailored to each child, teen, and family.

My Approach

I integrate approaches including Trauma-Focused Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (TF-CBT), Emotion-Focused Therapy, play-based interventions, and family systems perspectives.

Therapy is not one-size-fits-all. Together, we find approaches that fit each person's needs, personality, and goals.

Faith-Based Counseling

For clients who desire it, I offer Christian-integrated counseling that incorporates faith in healing, identity, relationships, and personal growth.
